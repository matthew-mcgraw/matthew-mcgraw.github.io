Teaching BB-8 to do stunts

Three progressively harder tasks for a Sphero BB-8 robot, trained from scratch using Proximal Policy Optimization inside the Webots physics simulator.

Each stage expands the robot's observation space, action space, and the complexity of what it needs to learn — from simple speed to obstacle avoidance to a timed mid-air jump.

The Stages

Stage 01

Go Fast

SP1
Drop or click to add video

Goal

Drive BB-8 as far from the center of the arena as possible, as fast as possible.

How it was achieved

The robot observes its x/y position and body rotation — 6 values. Each step, reward is proportional to distance from center. The agent learns to fling itself outward by coordinating pitch and yaw bursts. Trains with a compact 2-layer network and converges in a few hundred episodes.

6 obs 5 actions 2-layer

Stage 02

Go Around Obstacle

SP2

Goal

Navigate counter-clockwise around a box obstacle, through four checkpoints, into a goal zone — without hitting the walls or obstacle.

How it was achieved

Observation expands to 14 values: position, rotation, wall distances, and checkpoint flags. A shaped reward pulls the agent toward each successive waypoint, with bonuses at each gate and a lap-completion reward. A larger 3-layer / 128-unit network handles the more complex path.

14 obs 4 actions 3-layer · 128u

Stage 03

Jump Through Hoop

SP3

Goal

Drive BB-8 along a narrow ramp and execute a timed jump to pass through a hoop at the far end.

How it was achieved

Adds a jump action (upward impulse force) and expands observations to 17 values — including z-height, gyroscope, and accelerometer so the agent senses when it's airborne. The reward shapes forward progress. Jump timing is the critical challenge, discovered through thousands of trial-and-error episodes.

17 obs 5 actions Gyro + Accel
